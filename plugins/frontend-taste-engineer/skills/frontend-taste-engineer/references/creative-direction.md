# Creative direction selection

Choose direction from the brief, not from a fixed aesthetic list.

“Stunning” means exceptionally appropriate and well executed. It does not mean flashy, cinematic, loud, dark, experimental, gradient-heavy, or visually aggressive.

## Visual-intensity model

- `1 — institutional`: restrained, familiar, clarity-dominant.
- `2 — calm professional`: polished, trustworthy, subtly distinctive.
- `3 — distinctive product`: recognizable identity with controlled expression.
- `4 — expressive`: bold composition and memorable but purposeful motion.
- `5 — experimental`: unconventional, immersive, cinematic, or heavily art-directed.

Use the number internally unless it helps explain a decision. Infer it from domain, product type, audience, primary/secondary tasks, trust, risk, emotional objective, density, frequency, seriousness, brand/product maturity, accessibility, devices, interface archetype, experimental tolerance, and familiarity needs. Explicit user requirements override the starting range.

Typical starts: government/public-service and banking at 1; personal finance at 1–2; investment analytics, enterprise, and B2B infrastructure at 2–3; consumer productivity at 2–4; robotics at 3–4; campaigns at 4; personal expressive work and experimental portfolios at 4–5.

## Domain translation

- Finance: express quality through numeric typography, alignment, spacing, truthful tables/charts, clear units/freshness, refined states, and conservative motion. Never gamify risk or invent balances/returns.
- Healthcare, legal, education, and public service: prioritize clarity, reassurance, familiar interaction, plain language, accessibility, status, and recovery.
- Developer tools: prioritize technical precision, keyboard use, code/data typography, fast navigation, and strong loading/error behavior with low clutter.
- Premium and luxury: derive character from product positioning, materials, photography, brand type, composition, and interaction quality. Do not default to black, gold, huge serifs, empty space, or slow animation.
- Personal, artistic, entertainment, and campaign work: allow stronger typography, composition, humor, motion, and storytelling only when usable and concept-led.

Calm does not mean generic. Bold does not mean chaotic.

## Build the thesis

Write one sentence combining:

1. Product or narrative character.
2. Dominant hierarchy or composition.
3. Type and material stance.
4. Interaction or motion stance when material.
5. The user outcome the system must preserve.

The thesis must reject plausible alternatives. “Modern and clean” is not a thesis.

## Derive the vocabulary from the subject

Before choosing a style, inventory the subject's real materials, tools, language, environment, artifacts, behavior, and proof. Select only the two or three signals that clarify the audience and page job, then translate them into type, composition, imagery, structure, or one focal interaction. Record tempting signals you rejected because they were decorative, misleading, too literal, inaccessible, or too expensive.

Structural devices must carry a real relationship. Numbering implies order, an eyebrow classifies, a divider separates meanings, and a label identifies something users need to compare or navigate. If removing the device does not reduce comprehension, orientation, or task recovery, leave it out.

## Select on contextual axes

Choose positions only on axes that matter:

- Quiet–expressive.
- Dense–spacious.
- Editorial–instrumental.
- Familiar–novel.
- Warm–precise.
- Static–responsive.
- Documentary–abstract.

Tie each selected position to audience, message, trust, content maturity, and device context. A serious application can still be distinctive through proportion, language, and rhythm; it does not need decorative novelty. An expressive personal page can be theatrical without becoming illegible or motion-dependent.

## Minimalism is not vacancy

Minimalism removes decorative noise; it does not remove the hierarchy, proof, affordances, or relationships a user needs. Give every major gap a job: grouping, reading pace, focus, evidence framing, a task boundary, or a deliberate narrative beat. A spacious first view still needs specific context and a next step at ordinary laptop and mobile heights unless an earned editorial or cinematic pause says otherwise.

For a non-static direction, name at most three recurring motion roles—focal, state continuity, and direct feedback—and assign them to high-value moments. Do not animate every block into view or hide reading content until an observer fires. Reduced motion must preserve the same information and task path. Retrieve reviewed motion observations before direction lock; copy or install libraries/elements only after selection and the source-use gate.

## System rules

- Retrieve diversified evidence, compare candidate directions, then lock density, type, spacing, color roles, material, and first-viewport composition in `DESIGN.md` (`premium-quality-bar.md`).
- Let the primary message determine composition before choosing cards or sections.
- First viewport: brand-level signal, one headline, one support line, one CTA group, one dominant visual plane—not a dashboard of marketing modules.
- Create identity from two or three repeatable signals: type behavior, spatial rhythm, geometry, color role, material, illustration logic, or motion grammar.
- Trace those signals to the subject's own world rather than an unrelated trend or another brand's expression.
- Use contrast and variation intentionally. Repetition without hierarchy looks templated; novelty without a system looks arbitrary.
- Prefer authored typography, composition, and honest graphic abstraction to unrelated stock imagery. Do not use Inter, Roboto, Arial, or naked system-ui as the display identity.
- Use familiar patterns when they serve task comprehension. Replace them only when the alternative improves meaning.

**Hard reject** unless the thesis justifies an exception in one sentence: Inter/Roboto/Arial identity; purple-on-white or purple-to-indigo themes; centered hero + three equal feature cards as the whole page idea; glow/neon/glass stacks; pill/badge forests on the hero; decorative bento with no hierarchy; fake dashboards/metrics/testimonials; scroll-hijacking; animate-every-section-on-enter; stock smiling-team photography with no product truth; dark-cinematic or soft-UI defaults on calm high-trust products.

## Direction comparison

When the direction is uncertain, compare two materially different candidates using the real message. Score product fit, hierarchy, character, accessibility, responsive survival, performance cost, and integrity. Select one; do not average incompatible candidates.

## Verification

- A reviewer can infer the intended audience and tone from the rendered page.
- The brand test passes: removing the logo does not make the page interchangeable with any startup template.
- The direction survives narrow reflow and reduced motion.
- Each signature signal appears consistently but not everywhere.
- Each major gap has a stated hierarchy, pacing, evidence, or boundary role; useful context and the next action do not sit beyond a purposeless void.
- A non-static direction has at most three roles (focal, state, feedback), not incidental effects or universal scroll reveals.
- Removing gradients, shadows, and animation does not erase hierarchy.
- The result does not depend on fabricated proof or imagery.
- “Why this is not generic” is written and believable.
