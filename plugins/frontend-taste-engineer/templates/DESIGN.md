# Design lock

## Operating mode and evidence

Record the operating mode, new-build/redesign decision, supplied facts and quoted text, and separately labeled product assumptions. Note whether clarification occurred or judgment defaults were used.

## Product context

Document the user, job, trust level, device context, content/data maturity, existing system, and assumptions.

## Retrieved evidence before lock

List the compact evidence packet by record ID and source family. Separate core UX, source-derived design, copy, accessibility, responsive, integrity, and verification evidence. Record source-use restrictions.

## Candidate directions

Describe two or three materially different candidates across composition, typography, identity mechanism, color/surfaces, geometry, imagery/product media, motion posture, responsive behavior, and content shape. State the tradeoffs and evidence behind each.

## Selection

Name the selected direction, why it best fits the product constraints, and why the rejected candidates lost. This decision occurs after retrieval and comparison.

## Design thesis

Write one falsifiable sentence describing the product character, hierarchy, and visual/interaction stance.

## Direction and system

Record the subject vocabulary (real materials, tools, language, environment, and evidence), selected/rejected signals, composition, typography, color/surfaces, imagery/icons, motion, meaningful structural devices, action vocabulary, tokens/components, responsiveness, accessibility target, and performance budget. Link applied evidence IDs and explain rejected or overridden guidance.

## States and completion

List relevant loading, empty, error, success, offline, permission, destructive, recovery, and returning-user states plus evidence required for acceptance.

## Rendered refinement

Record desktop/mobile captures, the three highest-impact observed weaknesses, implemented fixes, revised captures, and remaining limitations.
