# Content lock

## Audience and task

Record the primary audience, job, decision, and action this content must support.

## Fact ledger

List supplied facts, verified product evidence, explicitly labeled assumptions, unresolved facts, prohibited claims, and source ownership. Never fill a gap with invented metrics, testimonials, integrations, guarantees, scarcity, or success states.

## Message hierarchy

Define the concrete outcome or premise, mechanism, proof, limitations or conditions, and specific next action in reading order.

## Page copy

Write the final headline, support, proof, section labels, body copy, actions, state copy, errors, recovery instructions, and relevant legal or trust language.

## Voice and anti-slop constraints

Record the vocabulary, tone boundaries, phrases to avoid, factual anchors, and reason every section exists. Prefer specific nouns and verbs over generic benefit abstractions.

## Responsive and localization behavior

Describe what remains visible at narrow widths, how action labels stay specific, acceptable line wrapping, localization expansion, long names, dates/numbers, RTL where relevant, and which safety or eligibility information may never be hidden merely to fit.

## Copy audit

Record factual-anchor omissions or introductions, vague calls to action, generic abstractions, repeated transitions/openings, and the corrections made. Treat heuristics as review signals, not authorship or quality proof.
